Create a schedule for reading all 73 books of the Catholic Bible. Choose an
inclusive ending date or a total number of reading days, then select one of two
reading orders.

The canonical-order plan reads from Genesis through Revelation. It keeps
chapters and the lettered additions to Esther intact, then distributes them as
evenly as possible. Fr. Adam's preferred reading order includes an Old
Testament, Gospel, and New Testament (Acts through Revelation) reading every
day. Those three tracks each remain in canonical order and are divided at
chapter or paragraph boundaries. Their estimated reading lengths are balanced
so that the Old Testament portion is longest, the New Testament portion next,
and the Gospel portion shortest.

Both plans use SBL book abbreviations and the chapter and verse numbering of the
New American Bible Revised Edition. Esther’s Greek additions appear as A–F;
Daniel includes the longer chapter 3 and chapters 13–14. For the preferred plan,
Gospel and New Testament paragraph boundaries and word-count estimates use
metadata derived from the public-domain [World English Bible Catholic edition](https://ebible.org/find/show.php?id=eng-web-c).
Old Testament portions stay at chapter boundaries and are estimated from verse
counts. No Bible text is copied into the plan. Everything is generated on your
device and no dates or plan data are sent anywhere. After generation, the page
shows the first few days so you can verify the schedule. The complete plan can
be downloaded as a CSV file, a Markdown checklist, or a simply formatted PDF
checklist; those files are also created entirely in your browser.
